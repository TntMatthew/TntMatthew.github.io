Discord Etiquette

This what I believe to be basic etiquette for public Discord servers. A lot of this can be applied to IRC too. In no particular order:
  • Don't be a asshat. This should be obvious. Yes, this is the internet, but you're speaking to real people. Not emotionless robots. Joking around and bantering is fine, but don't cross the line.
  • Don't ping people for no good reason. If you want to ask someone a question, or let them know of something important, pinging is usually fine, but doing it just 'cause is going to piss people off.
  • By extension, don't DM people for no reason. If you want to talk to someone privately, go ahead! That's what DMs are for, after all. But again, doing it just 'cause isn't funny. It is annoying.
  • Don't whine about nobody being around. If nobody's responding to your questions, that likely means that either nobody is around, or nobody knows the answer. Someone will get to it eventually. You may want to try asking again at a more active time.
  • Make your words count. If you need help with something, don't just say i need help. Make it clear what you want.
  • Don't spam. Mass-posting content irrelevant to the current discussion is not conversation. If you want to post a lot of images, consider using an imgur album instead of posting them in the chat. For links, you can <link> to disable embeds, which take up a lot of space and can be irritating in large quantities.
  • Don't overuse reactions. Using reactions in moderation is fine, but going overboard with them is not okay. Fortunately, never using them is always okay.
  • Don't overuse bots. Bots are fun, but spamming bot commands is just irritating for the rest of the server. Some servers may have a bots channel for heavy command usage - use that instead of a discussion channel.
  • Stay on topic. Discord servers often have multiple channels for a reason - use them.
  • Don't post the same question in every channel on the server. Post the question once in the correct channel, and wait. Sooner or later, you will get a response.
  • Don't use formatting or CAPS excessively. Occsional all-bold, all-caps or similar is okay... but don't do it all the time.
  • Don't ping admin or mod roles unless it's absolutely urgent. Again, this should be obvious. Alerting the whole mod/admin team for something minor is just unnecessary.
  • Don't advertise. If it's relevant, and the server rules allow it, it's fine to post some of your own content or where to find it every now and then, but don't spam it.
  • Grow a thick skin. You'll need it. On the Internet, people are assholes. Don't take anything personally.
  • Follow the server's rules. Once again, this should be obvious. While this list provides a general idea of how you should behave, every server is different. Follow the rules.
  • Don't do stuff just to annoy people. If someone doesn't like what you're doing and wants you to stop, stop.
That's all I can think of for now. I'll be updating this as I think of more stuff.

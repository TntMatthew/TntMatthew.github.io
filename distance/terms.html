Distance Community Glossary

This page is a glossary of every Distance community term you ever need to know ever. Thanks to MCrekna1, GammaFunction, Tiedye, pigpenguin and Californ1a for contributions/suggestions.

General

Adventure 2.0
Term used to describe the finished version of Adventure mode, which will be released when the game leaves early access.
Backer Playlist
A private SoundCloud playlist only available to Kickstarter backers, containing many more songs than the public one.
Boat Racing / Boating
Flipping the car over and driving upside-down.
Distathon
A 24-hour Distance charity livestream.
Distance Advent Calendar (DAC)
An event held during December, where community members get together to release custom levels on every day leading up to Christmas.
Drag Trigger
A gravity trigger that only changes drag.
Element
An obstacle or series of obstacles chained together between checkpoints. Most typically refers to transfers.
Experimental (Exp)
A secondary public release branch for the game, where new features not ready for prime time are tested in.
Flash Drive
The last known name of the planned second public car.
Full Boost
Verb for beating a level without releasing boost.
Nosestand
Propping the car up vertically on its nose.
On-Track
Term referring to the "intended" route of a level, i.e. no skips.
Refractor
The official name of the standard car.
Skeletron
The name of a special car that was temporaily made available during Halloween 2015, that lacked an on-screen HUD and had a double jump instead of wings.
Skip
An alternate route used in order to reach the end of a level faster, often using buildings as surfaces to drive on.
Spectrum
An unofficial modding framework that enables community made plugins to be loaded, allowing for additional functionality in the game.
Speedy Saturday (SS)
A weekly community get-together held every Saturday.
Sphagetti Track
Track that constantly loops back around and over itself, making it very prone to skips.
Stable
The main release branch of Distance.
Survive the Editor (STE)
A level creation competition that was held in October 2015.
Transfer
A jump and rotation between two roads or surfaces.
Unity'd
Verb for unusual behaviour, such as getting stuck in the ground, caused by Unity's inconsistent physics engine. Getting Unity'd often results in deaths.
2-Axis Transfer / Birotational Transfer
A transfer that requires using both the front/back and side thrusters.

Speedrunning

All Abilities Adventure (AAA)
A speedrun category where Adventure mode must be completed with the 'All Abilities in Adventure Mode' toggle enabled.
Boostless% / Noboost%
A joke speedrun category where Adventure mode must be completed without boosting.
Grip Fly
Using grip while upsidedown in the air to fly/hover. Often simply called grip, but obviously this is not to be confused with the grip ability itself.
Nerfboosting
A form of thruster boosting that instead uses two thrusters at once instead of four in seqeunce. Named after the player Nerf, who TBs in this style.
Wing Pumping
Repeatedly opening and closing wings in order to maintain or gain altitude, usually while doing rolls for cooldown.
Wing Turning
Opening wings briefly to conserve speed while changing direction.
S-Holding
A (now patched) physics quirk where holding the back thruster, which is by default bound to S on keyboard, increased speed.
Sprint SS
A speedrun category where all Sprint mode levels must be completed in a single sitting. Also called Officials SS.
Thruster Boosting (TB)
While upsidedown in the air, using all four thrusters in sequence repeadtedly in order to fly/hover.
Wheelie Boosting (WB)
Jumping, tilting up, and using grip repeatedly to increase speed. Basically the Distance equivalent of bunny hopping.

Distance Community Glossary

This page is a glossary of every Distance community term you ever need to know ever. Thanks to MCrekna1, GammaFunction and Californ1a for contributions/suggestions.

General

Adventure 2.0: Term used to describe the finished version of Adventure mode, which will be released when the game leaves early access.

Distathon: A 24-hour Distance charity livestream.

Distance Advent Calendar (DAC): An event held during December, where community members get together to release custom levels on every day leading up to Christmas.

Element: An obstacle or series of obstacles chained together between checkpoints. Most typically refers to transfers.

Experimental (Exp): A secondary public release branch for the game, where new features not ready for prime time are tested in.

Flash Drive: The last known name of the planned second public car.

Full Boost: Verb for beating a level without releasing boost.

Nosestand: Propping the car up vertically on its nose.

On-Track: Term referring to the "intended" route of a level, i.e. no skips.

Refractor: The official name of the standard car.

Skeletron: The name of a special car that was temporaily made available during Halloween 2015, that lacked an on-screen HUD and had a double jump instead of wings.

Skip: An alternate route used in order to reach the end of a level faster, often using buildings as surfaces to drive on.

Sphagetti Track: Track that constantly loops back around and over itself, making it very prone to skips.

Stable: The main release branch of Distance.

Survive the Editor (STE): A level creation competition that was held in October 2015.

Transfer: A jump and rotation between two roads or surfaces.

Unity'd: Verb for unusual behaviour, such as getting stuck in the ground, caused by Unity's inconsistent physics engine. Getting Unity'd often results in deaths.

2-Axis Transfer / Birotational Transfer: A transfer that requires using both the front and side thrusters.

Speedrunning

All Abilities Adventure (AAA): A speedrun category where Adventure mode must be completed with the 'All Abilities in Adventure Mode' toggle enabled.

Boostless% / Noboost%: A joke speedrun category where Adventure mode must be completed without boosting.

Grip Fly: Using grip while upsidedown in the air to fly/hover. Often simply called grip, but obviously this is not to be confused with the grip ability itself.

Wing Pumping: Repeatedly opening and closing wings in order to maintain or gain altitude, usually while doing rolls for cooldown.

S-Holding: A (now patched) physics quirk where holding the back thruster, which is by default bound to S on keyboard, increased speed.

Sprint SS: A speedrun category where all Sprint mode levels must be completed in a single sitting. Also called Officials SS.

Thruster Boosting (TB): While upsidedown in the air, using all four thrusters in sequence repeadtedly in order to fly/hover.

Wheelie Boosting (WB): Jumping, tilting up, and using grip repeatedly to increase speed. Basically the Distance equivalent of bunny hopping.

Acronyms/Abbreviations

BS: Broken Symmetry

LS: Lost Society

NS: Negative Space

GZ: Ground Zero

Machines / TTAM: The Thing About Machines

Observer: The Observer Effect

Falling: Falling Through

Mono: Monolith

Uncanny: Uncanny Valley

Adv: Adventure Mode

NRush/NR: Nitronic Rush, the student project that became Distance.

180: A f2c or c2f transfer.

f2c, c2f: A transfer between a floor and a ceiling.

f2w, w2f: A transfer between a floor and a wall.

w2c, c2w: A transfer between a wall and a ceiling.

w2w: A transfer between two walls.

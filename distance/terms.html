Distance Community Glossary

This page is a glossary of every Distance community term you ever need to know ever. Thanks to MCrekna1, GammaFunction, Tiedye, pigpenguin, dannyburke03 and Californ1a for contributions/suggestions.

General Terms

Adventure 2.0
Term used to describe the finished version of Adventure mode, which will be released when the game leaves early access.
All Abilities Adventure (AAA)
Adventure mode with the 'All Abilities in Adventure Mode' toggle enabled.
Backer Playlist
A private SoundCloud playlist only available to Kickstarter backers , containing many more songs than the public one.
Boat Racing / Boating
Flipping the car over and driving upside-down.
Boostless% / Noboost%
A joke speedrun category where Adventure mode must be completed without boosting.
Credit Card Skip
A skip that requires getting sliced small enough to sqeeuze through gaps that would be too small otherwise.
Damage Boosting (dboosting)
Intentionally bumping into obstacles such as saws or rollers to get a speed boost.
Dev Mode
A function that allows access to normally hidden work-in-progress objects not ready for prime time. Dev mode can be activated by applying a patch to the game; an easy method of doing this is installing Spectrum, as its patcher includes a patch for this by default.
Distance Advent Calendar (DAC)
An event held during December, where community members get together to release custom levels on every day leading up to Christmas.
Distance Rave Machine
A name for the music-synchronised RGB lighting setups that Refract use in their booths at conventions.
Distathon
A 24-hour Distance charity livestream.
Drag Trigger
A gravity trigger that only changes drag.
Element
An obstacle or series of obstacles chained together between checkpoints. Most typically refers to transfers.
Experimental (Exp)
A secondary public release branch for the game, where new features not ready for prime time are tested in.
Flash Drive
The last known name of the planned second public car.
Front Page
The top 15 spots on the global leaderboards.
Full Boost
Verb for beating a level without releasing boost.
Grip Fly
Using grip while upsidedown in the air to fly/hover. Often simply called grip, but obviously this is not to be confused with the grip ability itself.
Loading Hell
When a player gets stuck while loading a map during online play, preventing the race from starting.
Magnet Boosting (MB)
Using the car's magnetic downforce in order to gain a short burst of speed and height.
Nerfboosting
A form of thruster boosting that instead uses two thrusters at once instead of four in seqeunce. Named after the player Nerf, who TBs in this style.
Nonsense
A series of increasingly enigmatic puzzle maps that will cause your brain to dissolve.
Nosestand
Propping the car up vertically on its nose.
Nose Tapping
Pitching up/down and then back quickly to correct angles or go up/down while conserving speed.
NWBO
A series of extremely difficult air-control maps with wings disabled. Fittingly, it stands for "No Wings, Boost Only".
On-Track
Term referring to the "intended" route of a level, i.e. no skips.
Preservational Booster Sloth
An April Fools joke by Refract, where a new feature was showed off which put a sloth on top of the car, which slowed down time. Affectionately referred to in the community as Speedy.
Refractor
The official name of the standard car.
Sawboosting
Damage boosting off of a saw.
Skeletron
The name of a special car that was temporaily made available during Halloween 2015, that lacked an on-screen HUD and had a double jump instead of wings.
Skip
An alternate route used in order to reach the end of a level faster, often using buildings as surfaces to drive on.
Snow Top
The default car color scheme. Often also used as a term referring to players using this scheme, usually unskilled ones.
Spectrum
An unofficial modding framework that enables community made plugins to be loaded, allowing for additional functionality in the game.
Speedy Saturday (SS)
A weekly community get-together held every Saturday.
Sphagetti Track
Track that constantly loops back around and over itself, making it very prone to skips.
Sprint SS
A speedrun category where all Sprint mode levels must be completed in a single sitting. Also called Officials SS.
Stable
The main release branch of Distance.
Survive the Editor (STE)
A level creation competition that was held in October 2015.
Swiveling
A method of adjustment via air control, where the player briefly moves the jets in a circling motion in a certain direction, to help propel the player towards that direction. Should not be confused with TB.
S-Holding
A (now patched) physics quirk where holding the back thruster, which is by default bound to S on keyboard, increased speed.
The Travels of Distance
An April Fool's joke by Refract, where the car gained a cosmetic sword and shield, and breakable pots were randomly placed on the roads.
Thruster Boosting (TB)
While upsidedown in the air, using all four thrusters in sequence repeadtedly in order to fly/hover.
Thruster Rampdown
The curve where thrusters gradually lose strength as they're used while in the air.
Thruster Reset
Landing on a surface briefly to reset thruster power after thruster rampdown.
Tight Turning
Using thrusters to lean into corners for a sharper turn.
Transfer
A jump and rotation between two roads or surfaces.
Unity'd
Verb for unusual behaviour, such as getting stuck in the ground, caused by Unity's inconsistent physics engine. Getting Unity'd often results in deaths. Deaths from getting Unity'd are officialy referred to in-game as getting wrecked?.
Virus Spirit
The object name of the spooky flying ball in Ground Zero.
Wheelie Boosting (WB)
Jumping, tilting up, and using grip repeatedly to increase speed. Basically the Distance equivalent of bunny hopping.
Wing Fade-In
The delay between opening wings and when they start giving height.
Wing Pumping
Repeatedly opening and closing wings in order to maintain or gain altitude, usually while doing rolls for cooldown.
2-Axis Transfer / Birotational Transfer
A transfer that requires using both the front/back and side thrusters.
